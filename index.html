FAQ Section

What is succession planning?
Succession planning is the structured process of legally arranging the transfer of your business, wealth, assets, and responsibilities to your chosen beneficiaries. It ensures that your wishes are clearly documented and executed — avoiding legal disputes, conflicts, or delays.
How long does the succession planning process take?
A basic succession plan is typically completed within 7–14 working days. However, if you have a more comprehensive plan that involves trusts, business interests, or complex family structures, then it may take a little longer depending on the complexity and documentation involved.
What documents are needed for creating a will or trust?
To create a legally valid will or trust, you’ll typically need:
  • 1) An organized list of all your assets and liabilities
  • 2) Details of your beneficiaries (family members, organizations, etc.)
  • 3) Identification documents (like Aadhaar/PAN)
  • 4) Nomination or joint holding details, if applicable
  • 5) A trusted executor or trustee’s information
